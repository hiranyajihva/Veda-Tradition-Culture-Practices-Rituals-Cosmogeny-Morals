Āpastamba’s Prescriptions for the Uninitiated
By Híraṇyajihva


Following the Āpastamba-dharma-sūtrá of the Tāittirīya şā́khā

Nowadays, the importance of the upa-nayana or the brahmanical initiation ceremony has drastically declined, to now become a small ritual performed a couple years,
or in most cases, a couple days before the vi-vāha to ensure that the groom can be called a ‘brāhmaṇa’. 
Even those that are performed many years before or even during the prescribed ages that Āpastamba enforces are not accompanied by actual vedic education (śikṣā),
though this is beyond the scope of this post. 

Those few who are vedically ‘inclined’ may wonder what the expiation (which is necessary) is for getting the upa-nayana done beyond the age range that Āpastamba prescribes.
Āpastamba describes this in Praśṇa I, Paṭala 1, Kaṇḍa 1 and Kaṇḍa 2. There are mainly 3 cases for the expiation viz. 
The late initiates of brāhmaṇa-s, The brahmahasam̐stuta or the brahmin-slayers, and the śmaśānasam̐stuta or the cremation grounds. 


The Proper Age for Initiation According to Āpastamba

वसन्ते ब्राह्मणमुपनयीत ग्रीष्मे राजन्यँ शरदि वैश्यं गर्भाष्टमेषु ब्राह्मणं गर्भेकादशेषु राजन्यं गर्भद्वादशेषु वैश्यम् ||१९||

19. Let him initiate a Brāhmaṇa in spring, a Kṣatriya in summer, a Vaiśya in autumn, a Brāhmaṇa in the eighth year after conception, 
a Kṣatriya in the eleventh year after conception, (and) a Vaiśya in the twelfth after conception.

 अथ काम्यानि ||२०||
20. Now (follows the enumeration of the years to be chosen) for the fulfillment of some (particular) wish.
 सप्तमे ब्रह्मवर्चसका- मम् ||२१||
21. (Let him initiate) a person desirous of excellence in sacred learning in his seventh year.

 अष्टम आयुष्कामम् ||२२||
22. A person desirous of long life in his eighth year.

 नवमे तेजस्कामम् ||२३|| 
23. A person desirous of manly vigor in his ninth year.

दशमेऽन्नाद्य-कामम् ||२४||
24. A person desirous of food in his tenth year.

 एकादश इन्द्रियकामम् ||२५||
25. A person desirous of strength in his eleventh year.

 द्वादशे पशुकामम् ||२६||
26. A person desirous of cattle in his twelfth year.


The Maximum Age for Initiation According to Āpastamba

आ षोडशाद्ब्राह्मणस्यानात्यय आ द्वाविंशात्क्षत्रियस्या चतुर्विशाद्वैश्यस्य यथा व्रतेषु समर्थः स्याद्यानि वक्ष्यामः ||२७||

27. There is no dereliction (of duty, if the initiation takes place), in the case of a Brāhmaṇa before the completion of the sixteenth year, 
in the case of a Kṣatriya before the completion of the twenty-second year, in the case of a Vaiśya before the completion of the twenty-fourth year. 
(Let him be initiated at such an age) that he may be able to perform the duties, which we shall declare below. [1]



[1] Note that tradition counts the years starting from conception, so the brāmana’s limit is 15 years and 3 months, 
a Kṣatriya’s is 21 years and 3 months and a Vaiśya’s is 23 years and 3 months from birth. 

The Late Initiates of Brāhmaṇa-s


अतिक्रान्ते सावित्र्या: काल ऋतुं त्रैविद्यकं ब्रह्मचर्यं चरेत् || २८ || 
28. If the proper time for the initiation has passed, he shall observe for the space of two months the duties of a student, 
as observed by those who are studying the three Vedas.

 अथोपनय- नम्  || २९ || 
29. After that he may be initiated.

 ततः संवत्सरमुदकोपस्पर्शनम्  ||  ३० || 
30. After that he shall bathe (daily) for one year.

 अथाध्याप्यः || ३१ || 
31. After that he may be instructed.


As we can see from the above sūtra-s Āpastamba declares that one whose upa-nayana has not been performed before the maximum age as mentioned above in sūtra 27, 
has to observe the “duties of a student” for one season, taken as two months. 
What these ‘duties’ are is defined by Āpastamba in the next Kaṇḍa from sūtra 18 all the way to sūtra 38 of Kaṇḍa 11 of Paṭala 3. 
Some of these, though, are not applicable to the uninitiated, for example sūtra-s 19-21 which are rules pertaining to the behavior of the student in the presence of his teacher.
