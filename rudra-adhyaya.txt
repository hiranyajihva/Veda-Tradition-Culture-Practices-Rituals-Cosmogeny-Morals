The śrī rudram as referred to nowadays consists of two parts viz. the namakam and the camakam.

The namakam is based on is based on the 11 anuvāka-s of Tāittirīya-saṁhitā́ 4.5 and the camakam is based on is based on the first 11 anuvāka-s of Tāittirīya-saṁhitā́ 4.7.

Note that the śrī rudram is not a 'vedic' chant, but rather a chant based on the parts of the Tāittirīya-saṁhitā́ given above. Extra mantra-s have been added.

The verse you are refering to "oṃ namaste astu bhagavan viśveśvarāya mahādevāya tryambakāya tripurāntakāya trikālāgnikālāya kālāgnirudrāya nīlakaṇṭhāya mṛtyuṃjayāya sarveśrvarāya sadāśivāya śrīman mahādevāya namaḥ" is not part of the original rudra-praśna or the rudrādhyāya. It is a later addition.
